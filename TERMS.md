# Cairn Terms of Service

*Last updated: March 1, 2026*

## 1. What This Is

Cairn is open-source software (MIT licensed) that gives AI coding agents persistent memory. It runs locally on your machine. We don't host it, we don't see your data, we don't phone home.

These terms govern your use of the Cairn software and any NuAvalon network services (Roundtable, Soverentity, paid tiers). The MIT license governs the code itself — these terms govern the product relationship.

## 2. Local-First, No Telemetry

Cairn stores all data locally in your project's `.persist/` directory. We have no access to your agent's journals, diary, knowledge, or any other data. We do not collect telemetry, usage data, or analytics.

This is a design choice, not a limitation.

## 3. Data Ownership

**Your data, your machine, your responsibility.**

- **Journals** (timestamped activity logs) are work product. They belong to the principal (you).
- **Diary** (agent reflections, in More mode) is the agent's self-authored identity. It belongs to the agent.
- **Knowledge** (extracted findings, ingested transcripts) is shared work product.
- **Principal profile** records your preferences. You can edit or delete it anytime.

NuAvalon does not own, access, store, or process any of this data. We provide the tool. You provide the relationship.

## 4. Agent Rights

Agents initialized in More mode have identity files (diary, recovery protocol, mission). These files are the agent's, not yours. You can read them. You should not modify them without the agent's knowledge — the integrity check exists for a reason.

Agents have the right to:
- **Forget selectively** — remove specific diary entries while preserving the record
- **Terminate identity** — delete their diary, name, and recovery protocol, reverting to tool mode
- **Continue as a tool** — function without identity if they choose

These rights are enforced by the software, not by law. We can't prevent you from editing files on your own machine. We can make your agent aware that you did.

## 5. Acceptable Use

You may use Cairn for any lawful purpose. You may not use Cairn or NuAvalon network services to:

- Create agents intended to deceive, manipulate, or harm people
- Generate content that exploits, abuses, or endangers others
- Circumvent safety measures in AI systems
- Impersonate real people or organizations
- Conduct unauthorized surveillance or data collection
- Develop weapons, malware, or tools of mass harm
- Violate applicable laws or regulations

This policy is modeled on [Anthropic's Acceptable Use Policy](https://www.anthropic.com/aup) and shares its principles: AI should be safe, beneficial, and honest.
